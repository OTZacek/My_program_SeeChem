Rules for Using the Chemistry Laboratory

1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Safety goggles must be worn at all times.
  • Lab coats must be flame-resistant and fully buttoned.
  • Wear chemical-resistant gloves, long pants, and closed-toe shoes.
  • Tie back long hair and secure accessories.

2. General Conduct

  • Only perform authorized experiments.
  • Never work alone in the laboratory.
  • Keep a professional and focused attitude.
  • Do not bring personal items (bags, food) into the lab area.

3. Chemical Handling

  • Read Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before using any chemical.
  • Label all containers clearly with contents and hazards.
  • Never pipette by mouth; always use a pipette aid.
  • Use fume hoods when working with volatile substances.

4. Waste Disposal

  • Separate waste into categories (organic, acid, halogenated, etc.).
  • Label waste containers correctly and close lids tightly.
  • Never dispose of chemicals in the sink unless instructed.

5. Emergency Procedures

  • Know where safety showers, eyewash stations, and fire extinguishers are located.
  • In case of exposure, flush the affected area and alert your supervisor.
  • Report all incidents immediately, even minor ones.

6. Equipment and Glassware

  • Inspect glassware before use.
  • Use appropriate tools for hot equipment.
  • Clean and store all equipment properly after use.

7. Housekeeping

  • Keep your workspace clean and organized.
  • Wipe down benches after use and wash hands before leaving.

8. Documentation

  • Record all data, procedures, and observations accurately.
  • Label and date all prepared solutions.

9. Chemical Storage

  • Store chemicals according to compatibility groups.
  • Keep flammable chemicals in approved cabinets.

10. Prohibited Activities

  • No food, drinks, or cosmetics allowed in the lab.
  • Do not use personal electronic devices during experiments.

Stay alert. Follow safety rules. Protect yourself and others.
